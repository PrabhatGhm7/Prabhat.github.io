About

CS meets the cockpit

I'm a computer engineering graduate from Pokhara University with a deep interest in the intersection of machine learning and aviation safety. My capstone research on "Extending GPWS through Probabilistic Machine Learning for Early CFIT Detection in Challenging Approach Environments" made one thing clear these are exactly the problem space I want to spend the next chapter of my career in.

Nepal's mountainous terrain makes its aviation environment one of the most challenging in the world. I believe data-driven approaches can meaningfully shift the odds, and I'm working toward a Masters in Applied Aviation Safety to build the technical foundation to do that.

Beyond research, I've spent time teaching computer science at the secondary level — an experience that has sharpened how I communicate complex ideas clearly, which I think makes for better research and better collaboration.

"Every safe flight is a system working as designed. My purpose is clear, to help build an aviation system where safety is the standard, notthe exception."

Capstone Research
Extending GPWS Through Probabilistic Machine Learning for Early CFIT Detection

Research into probabilistic ML methods to improve aviation safety systems, targeting Controlled Flight Into Terrain (CFIT) — one of aviation's most persistent fatal accident categories. Applied temporal pattern analysis and rare-event modelling to push the boundaries of ground proximity warning capability.

  • Achieved early warnings ~100 seconds before potential collision events
  • Used simulation-based data augmentation to address severe class imbalance
  • Combined temporal pattern analysis with probabilistic risk prediction
  • Prototyped and validated using simulated aviation flight data
